Co-op Work Term Report

Elliott Rahming · Bachelor of Computing (Software Engineering Co-op), University of Guelph

Term: Sep 2025 - Dec 2025 · Employer: City of Guelph — IT Service Desk Support (Co-op)

Troubleshooting Documentation Customer Support Service Desk Enterprise Tools

Introduction

In this work term, I supported end users across multiple departments by troubleshooting hardware and software issues, documenting incidents, and improving support workflows. This report summarizes what I worked on, what I learned, and how the experience strengthened my communication, problem-solving, and technical proficiency in an enterprise IT environment.

About the Employer

During Fall 2025, I completed my work term with the City of Guelph in an IT Service Desk Support role. The City supports a wide range of internal departments and relies on stable IT systems to keep day-to-day operations running smoothly. The service desk serves as a front line for issue intake, troubleshooting, and escalation when necessary.

This environment relates directly to computing through real-world incident management, troubleshooting, identity and access workflows, and documentation practices. It also provided experience working with enterprise tools and processes, where clarity, repeatability, and good communication directly affect user experience and operational efficiency.

Goals / Learning Outcomes

The following learning goals guided my work term and were evaluated through day-to-day performance, feedback, and my ability to work independently while maintaining high-quality support.

Goal 1 — Communication (Oral Communication)

Strengthen my ability to communicate clearly and professionally with both technical and non-technical staff, ensuring information is conveyed accurately in conversations, meetings, and interactions.

Action Plan

  • Use clear and professional language when interacting with users and team members
  • Follow up consistently and ask clarifying questions when needed

Measure of Success

Receive positive feedback from supervisors/users/colleagues and demonstrate the ability to explain technical concepts in a way non-technical staff can understand.

Reflection

I made a conscious effort to use understandable language and keep explanations concise, especially when speaking with users who did not have technical backgrounds. When issues were highly technical, I focused on giving a clear high-level overview. A key area for improvement is organizing my thoughts more effectively so my explanations are less fragmented and sound more professional and articulate.

Goal 2 — Critical & Creative Thinking (Problem Solving)

Develop stronger analytical and troubleshooting skills by applying structured approaches to resolving technical issues, identifying root causes, and implementing effective solutions.

Action Plan

  • Take ownership of troubleshooting tickets and apply diagnostic steps
  • Collaborate with colleagues when challenges arise and document solutions for reuse

Measure of Success

Independently resolve approximately 2-3 incidents/service requests per day and successfully escalate or document complex issues with clear resolution details.

Reflection

Over the term, I became significantly more independent in diagnosing issues and gained a stronger understanding of the technologies used at the City of Guelph. I improved my ability to handle calls and in-person support confidently. While I sometimes made incorrect initial decisions or misinterpreted issues, I worked to correct them quickly once identified. I can improve by reducing resolution time and reaching out for help earlier when appropriate. I consistently documented resolution details for less-common problems to support future troubleshooting.

Goal 3 — Technological Literacy

Expand my knowledge and proficiency with the City of Guelph's IT systems, tools, and platforms, and apply this understanding to perform tasks efficiently and independently.

Action Plan

  • Build proficiency with tools such as Cherwell, SharePoint, Active Directory, VPN, and related systems
  • Learn by shadowing colleagues and practicing standard tasks

Measure of Success

  • Use core applications independently
  • Complete tasks like login resets, permissions changes, and troubleshooting with minimal supervision

Reflection

I gained substantial experience with corporate IT systems and developed a stronger understanding of what happens behind the scenes in tools that are often abstracted for end users. This came from solving issues across multiple teams (e.g., network, server, asset control). While there are still tools/features I am not fully familiar with, I made an effort to learn the correct steps as incidents arose. I can complete most tasks I have permissions for without supervision, but I sometimes needed more clarity when expectations required inference. When I made mistakes, I focused on learning from them and avoiding repeats.

Job Description / What I Worked On

My role focused on helping staff resolve technical issues efficiently while ensuring the work was documented clearly for future reference. I worked with a mix of self-directed tasks, tickets, and direct user interactions (phone/in-person).

Incident & Service Request Handling

  • Owned tickets end-to-end when possible: intake, diagnosis, resolution, and follow-up
  • Escalated complex issues with clear notes when required
  • Documented resolution details, especially for less-common problems

User Support & Communication

  • Explained technical issues in understandable language for non-technical users
  • Asked clarifying questions and provided concise, actionable instructions
  • Improved professionalism and clarity in live support interactions

Enterprise Tools & Workflows

  • Built proficiency with tools such as Cherwell, SharePoint, Active Directory, and VPN workflows
  • Completed standard tasks like login resets and permission changes (based on access)
  • Learned by shadowing colleagues and applying steps independently as new issues appeared

This role strengthened practical problem-solving and reinforced the value of repeatable processes, accurate documentation, and clear communication — especially when supporting time-sensitive operations.

Results, Testing Mindset, and Problem Solving

My key outcomes were improved independence, clearer documentation, and stronger troubleshooting habits. I also identified specific areas to improve next term: faster resolution time and reaching out earlier when appropriate.

  • More independent troubleshooting: I became more comfortable diagnosing issues on my own and handling service desk interactions with confidence.
  • Better documentation quality: I consistently documented resolution details so that solutions could be reused by others in the future.
  • Clearer communication: I focused on concise explanations for non-technical users and identified a personal improvement area: organizing my thoughts to sound more articulate and professional.

Conclusion

This work term strengthened three core areas that will carry into future co-op roles: clear technical communication, structured troubleshooting, and practical proficiency with enterprise IT tools and workflows. I learned how important documentation and consistency are in a real support environment, and I gained confidence handling issues independently. Moving forward, my main improvement targets are reducing resolution time and communicating complex ideas more smoothly by organizing my thoughts more effectively.

Acknowledgments

I would like to thank my supervisor and the IT Service Desk team at the City of Guelph for their support, guidance, and willingness to answer questions as I learned new systems and processes. I also appreciate the colleagues across related teams who helped with escalations and provided context that improved my understanding of how enterprise IT operations work behind the scenes.
